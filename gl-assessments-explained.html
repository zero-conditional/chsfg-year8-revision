Careful background

What can and cannot be said about the GL assessments

This page adds background for students and parents who want more context. It does not replace the CHSFG booklet, which remains the main reference point for what students at this school will sit.

Important caveat

Start with the school booklet

The booklet tells CHSFG students and parents what the school expects students to sit in May 2026. Anything on this page is extra context taken from official GL Assessment websites and support pages. It is background information, not a replacement for school instructions.

What GL is

GL Assessment is a UK assessment provider

GL Assessment publishes standardised tests used by schools to measure attainment and progress. The official GL material that looks most relevant here is the Progress Test Series, covering English, Maths, and Science.

Official source: GL Progress Test Series

Most likely match

Why this seems relevant to CHSFG

The school booklet's GL English, GL Maths, and GL Science descriptions line up quite closely with GL Assessment's published descriptions of PTE (Progress Test in English), PTM (Progress Test in Maths), and PTS (Progress Test in Science).

This is a careful match, not a certainty. The school booklet still comes first.

Official GL English

PTE

  • Focuses on spelling, grammar, punctuation, and reading comprehension.
  • Uses fiction and non-fiction or information texts.
  • GL says it is designed as an attainment test with standardised reporting.

Sources: PTE product page, About PTE

Official GL Maths

PTM

  • Measures maths skills and knowledge, reasoning, and problem solving.
  • GL says it includes mental maths for students aged 8 and over.
  • Used for benchmarking attainment and tracking progress over time.

Sources: PTM product page, About PTM

Official GL Science

PTS

  • Measures science knowledge, understanding, application, and working scientifically.
  • GL says it samples science attainment and scientific skills.
  • Also used for standardised reporting and progress tracking.

Sources: PTS product page, About PTS

What GL says

How the Progress Test Series is normally used

  • Schools use it to benchmark attainment against national data.
  • Schools can use it for baselining or for measuring progress over time.
  • GL offers paper and digital versions for different levels.
  • Reports are designed for teachers, groups, individuals, and sometimes parents.

Source: Progress Test Series overview

What not to assume

School use may still vary

  • Schools can choose different levels or forms of the tests.
  • Schools may adapt timing, scheduling, or preparation around their own assessment calendar.
  • The exact paper a Year 8 class sits should not be assumed from GL's public pages alone.
  • For CHSFG students and parents, the booklet remains the final word on format and preparation.

Best way to use this

Use the extra context carefully

For CHSFG students and parents, the best order is: school booklet first, subject page second, explainer page third. This page is most useful when a little more background is needed on why the GL papers seem broader than a normal single-subject class test.
