Draft page

Practice papers and questions

This page is a draft guide to what can be practised for CHSFG Year 8 exams. It is built around the school booklet, the published curriculum roadmaps, and public revision resources.

Important caveat

There are no official CHSFG practice papers in the booklet

The booklet handed to students and parents does not include full practice papers. Instead, it points students toward practice questions, example questions, revision booklets, lesson resources, school systems such as Class Charts, SharePoint, Sparx, Sparx Reader, and Teaching Gadget, plus public sites such as BBC Bitesize.

That means this page should be read as a guide to practice routes, not as a bank of official past papers.

This creates an awkward middle ground. These are Year 8 school tests, so GCSE past papers are usually too far ahead, while 11+ or grammar-school style papers are often the wrong type of preparation. The safest route is to stay close to the booklet, subject content, and CHSFG curriculum coverage.

School-linked first

Best starting point for CHSFG students

  • Use the subject page on this site alongside the school booklet.
  • Check whether the booklet names a school system such as Class Charts, SharePoint, Sparx, Sparx Reader, or Teaching Gadget.
  • Use the school curriculum page to confirm the wider Year 8 sequence for that subject.
  • Then use public question banks only after the school-linked route is clear.

Why this is tricky

Not GCSE papers and not 11+ papers

For many subjects, there is no single public bank of papers that matches CHSFG Year 8 closely. These assessments sit between two better-known worlds:

  • GCSE papers are often too specialised, too exam-board-specific, or simply too advanced.
  • 11+ and grammar-school style papers are designed for selective-entry testing, not Year 8 classroom learning.
  • Internal school tests are usually built around what pupils have actually studied that year.

That is why this site treats the booklet and CHSFG curriculum as the anchor, then uses outside resources more selectively.

Official providers

Where official sources do help

Official providers can still be useful, but mostly for background and question style rather than a ready-made bank of matching papers.

  • GL Assessment is the closest official reference point for the GL English, GL Maths, and GL Science papers.
  • Pearson KS3 Maths support can be useful for maths practice that stays closer to Key Stage 3 than GCSE papers do.
  • AQA KS3 Science can help with science knowledge and assessment style, but it is not a CHSFG paper bank.

For CHSFG students, these sources should be treated as secondary to the school booklet and school-linked resources.

Subject practice

What can actually be practised

English

The booklet points to the preparation lesson, Class Charts, previous creative writing, and descriptive writing skills.

  • Practise image-based planning activities followed by one description.
  • Reuse teacher feedback from earlier creative writing.
  • Use Oak National, BBC Bitesize, and Class Charts rather than looking for a full paper.

Maths

The strongest practice route is clearly Sparx, backed up by the Year 8 curriculum mapping PDFs.

  • Use weekly Sparx homework, Independent Learning, and XP Boost.
  • Use the autumn, spring, and summer term mapping PDFs to target exact Year 8 objectives.
  • Build short mixed sets instead of waiting for a mock paper.

Science

The booklet points to practice questions and the Year 8 roadmap gives a clean topic list.

  • Use core questions from each topic.
  • Practise short-answer and data interpretation questions.
  • Use BBC Bitesize, Grade Gorilla, Seneca, and Sparx Science for topic practice.

History

The booklet gives the clearest school-linked practice source here: the KS3 revision booklet pages.

  • Use the named revision booklet pages for abolition and civil rights, technological revolution, changing nature of war, and My Voice Matters.
  • Practise multiple-choice recall.
  • Practise short source usefulness responses with own knowledge and provenance.

Geography

The booklet focuses on landscape systems, crime and power, fieldwork, and case-study knowledge.

  • Practise 1- to 6-mark questions on named case studies.
  • Do map, graph, climate-graph, and fieldwork skills questions.
  • Use Internet Geography, BBC Bitesize, and Newsround to support case-study recall.

Religious Studies

The booklet gives a clear structure for 4-mark and 6-mark answers, which makes question practice more important than paper-hunting.

  • Practise multiple-choice recall on key terms and topics.
  • Practise 4-mark point-explain-example responses.
  • Practise 6-mark answers linking points to a teaching, quote, parable, miracle, or religious story.

Modern Foreign Languages

The booklet focuses on translation and writing, and the roadmap shows the Year 8 themes of city, technology and media, and holidays.

  • Do short translation tasks.
  • Practise extended writing using sentence builders.
  • Use Memrise, Quizlet, Conjuguemos, and BBC Bitesize for vocabulary and grammar practice.

Music

The booklet gives three clear practice routes: listening, factual recall, and notation reading.

  • Use the music booklet and Teaching Gadget for topic revision.
  • Practise note reading and chords with musictheory.net.
  • Do short listening responses using musical vocabulary.

Art

The booklet points more toward practical studies than written papers, and the Year 8 roadmap gives a wider picture of portraiture and identity work.

  • Do timed eye studies and blending strips.
  • Practise short reflective writing after each study.
  • Use the roadmap themes to widen sketchbook-style practice beyond the exam task.

GL practice

How to practise for the GL papers without past papers

GL English

  • Use the dedicated prep lesson materials and example questions if they were brought home.
  • Use BBC Bitesize for SPaG.
  • Use Sparx Reader and short comprehension tasks on fiction and non-fiction texts.

GL Maths

  • Use short mental maths bursts.
  • Use mixed reasoning and problem-solving questions from Maths Genie, Corbettmaths, and DrFrost.
  • Focus on choosing the method, not only carrying out the calculation.

GL Science

  • Use mixed Year 7 and Year 8 recall questions.
  • Practise graph and table interpretation.
  • Include working scientifically vocabulary and method questions.

Curriculum context

How the roadmaps help

The school curriculum PDFs do not provide practice papers, but they do help confirm what Year 8 studies at CHSFG. That is especially useful when choosing public questions so time is not wasted on the wrong topics.

  • Maths term mappings are especially detailed and can be turned into topic checklists.
  • Science, Geography, History, MFL, Art, and English roadmaps help confirm Year 8 coverage.
  • The booklet remains the main exam guide; the roadmaps help with topic selection.
