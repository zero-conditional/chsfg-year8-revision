Why these exams matter

They show what is secure, and what still needs attention

The booklet is right to treat these exams seriously. They are not only about grades for one week in May. They help show which knowledge and skills from Year 8 are secure, which areas need more practice, and where a student may need a clearer revision plan.

That matters because Year 8 sits in the middle of Key Stage 3. A good revision routine now can make later assessments feel more manageable rather than more sudden.

Best-practice view

Revision is most useful when it is planned, checked, and kept manageable

  • Education evidence strongly supports strategies that help pupils plan, monitor, and evaluate their own learning.
  • Low-stakes checking and feedback help reveal gaps before they become bigger problems.
  • Breaking revision into smaller tasks and planning ahead can also reduce stress around exam time.

Further reading: EEF on metacognition and self-regulation, EEF on feedback, NHS Every Mind Matters on stress

What this means at home

The aim is steady preparation, not panic revision

  • Use the booklet and timetable early so revision starts before the final week.
  • Focus on the next paper, the next weak topic, and one manageable practice task.
  • Use short self-checks to see what has stuck instead of only rereading notes.
  • Treat the exams as a chance to build habits that will help in later years as well.

About this site

Where the information comes from

The exam dates, subject list, and main revision guidance on this site are taken from the CHSFG Year 8 exam booklet for May 2026. The site then reorganises that material into pages that are easier to scan at home.

What is on this site?

Booklet subjects plus GL assessments

  • Subject guides for Art, Computing, English, Geography, History, Maths, MFL, Music, RS, and Science
  • GL English, GL Maths, and GL Science support pages
  • Practice ideas and self-check prompts added around the booklet guidance
  • A separate practice papers and questions page with clear caveats
  • A simplified timetable and a separate exam tips page

What to revise first

Start with the next paper and the next weak area

  • Maths and GL Maths reward regular question practice.
  • Science, Geography, and RS benefit from facts plus exam-style responses.
  • English, History, and MFL improve through repeated short writing tasks.
