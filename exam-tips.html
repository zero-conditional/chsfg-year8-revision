Revision support

Exam tips for a calmer revision routine

These ideas are shaped for families who want something realistic rather than overwhelming.

Planning

Keep school-night revision realistic

  • Plan short blocks rather than one long session.
  • Use 50 minutes of work and a 10-minute break.
  • On school nights, keep it to no more than about 2 hours.
  • Mix subjects across the week so one subject does not take over.

Memory

Use retrieval, not just re-reading

  • Read a small chunk, cover it, then recall it.
  • Use flashcards for facts, formulas, and keywords.
  • Practise questions regularly with a mark scheme where possible.
  • Revisit the same material a few days later to strengthen memory.

Wellbeing

Protect confidence during exam season

  • Eat, drink, sleep, and take breaks properly.
  • Do not compare every answer with others after an exam.
  • Reward progress, even when it feels small.
  • Keep the next revision step simple and clear.

Sample weekly rhythm

A gentle plan that families can actually keep up

  1. Monday: one weaker topic in Maths or Science
  2. Tuesday: English planning or reading practice
  3. Wednesday: GL skills practice
  4. Thursday: one short mixed review session
  5. Weekend: one longer catch-up block plus one evening off

Exam day checklist

Keep the morning simple

  • Pack pens, pencil, ruler, and calculator the night before.
  • Read each question carefully and notice the command words.
  • Start with a steady pace rather than rushing to finish first.
  • Leave time for a final check where the subject allows it.
